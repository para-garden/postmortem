## Writing principles to remember

- Earth, no people. Don't explain why. Don't gesture at why.
- No narrator. No "you walk into the room." Things are. Verbs of state.
- The dread is fear of the unknown — who built this, where did they go, what happened. Never asked, never answered.
- No safe distance. No archaeology framing. The food on the table looks like yesterday because it is yesterday, indefinitely.
- Stay-concrete and stay-totally-flat are both valid. "The cities are empty" lands. "A worn handle" lands. Both are the project. Neither is the rule.
- Don't aestheticize. Don't lyricize. Don't earn the dread with craft. State the world; the reader does the rest.
- The reader being human is the entire mechanism. They are the missing referent. They are the only agent in the world.
